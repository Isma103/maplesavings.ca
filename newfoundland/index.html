Newfoundland & Labrador RRSP & FHSA Tax Calculator — FAQ

What are the Newfoundland and Labrador income tax brackets for 2025?

NL has eight provincial tax brackets for 2025: 8.7% up to $44,192; 14.5% to $88,382; 15.8% to $157,792; 17.8% to $220,910; 19.8% to $282,214; 20.8% to $564,429; 21.3% to $1,128,858; and 21.8% over $1,128,858. The NL basic personal amount is $11,067 for 2025.

What is the combined marginal tax rate in Newfoundland at $75,000 income?

At $75,000 in Newfoundland and Labrador, your combined federal and provincial marginal tax rate is approximately 35% (20.5% federal + 14.5% NL provincial).

What is the top combined marginal tax rate in Newfoundland and Labrador?

Newfoundland and Labrador's highest combined marginal rate is approximately 54.8% on income above $1,128,858, combining the 33% federal top rate with NL's 21.8% top provincial rate — one of the highest in the country on the very highest incomes.

Can I use the FHSA as a Newfoundland and Labrador resident?

Yes. NL residents can fully benefit from the FHSA. Contributions are deductible from both federal and NL provincial taxable income, and qualifying withdrawals for a first home purchase are tax-free. The annual limit is $8,000 in your first year (up to $16,000 with carry-forward room), with a $40,000 lifetime cap.
