Quebec RRSP & FHSA Tax Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

Why are Quebec taxes different from other provinces?

Quebec administers its own income tax system through Revenu Québec, separately from the CRA. Quebec residents receive a 16.5% federal tax abatement (paying less federal tax), but Quebec's own provincial rates are among the highest in Canada. This calculator shows estimates based on nominal rates — for precise Quebec figures consult Revenu Québec or a tax professional.

What is Quebec's combined marginal tax rate at $75,000?

At $75,000 of employment income in Quebec, the nominal combined marginal tax rate is approximately 39.5% — 20.5% federal plus 19% Quebec provincial. After applying the 16.5% federal abatement that Quebec residents receive, your effective rate will be lower. Use this calculator as an estimate and verify with Revenu Québec.

What is an FHSA and how much can I contribute?

The First Home Savings Account (FHSA) is for first-time homebuyers. In your first year, you can contribute up to $8,000. Unused room carries forward (up to $8,000), allowing up to $16,000 in a carry-forward year. The lifetime maximum is $40,000. Contributions are fully tax-deductible and withdrawals for a first home purchase are tax-free.

What is the RRSP contribution limit for 2025 and 2026?

The 2025 RRSP limit is $32,490 (or 18% of your 2024 earned income, whichever is lower). For 2026, the limit increased to $33,810. Unused room carries forward indefinitely. Your personal limit is on your CRA Notice of Assessment or in My CRA Account.

Can I contribute to both an RRSP and FHSA in the same year as a Quebec resident?

Yes — Quebec residents can contribute to both an RRSP and an FHSA in the same tax year. Both are deductible from your Quebec provincial taxable income as well as federal. This calculator shows combined RRSP + FHSA savings estimates for Quebec above.

Is my personal data safe when using MapleSavings?

Yes. MapleSavings runs entirely in your browser — your income, RRSP room, FHSA room, and all other inputs are never sent to any server or third party. All calculations happen locally on your device and nothing is stored or tracked.

Are these Quebec tax calculations accurate?

This calculator uses 2025 CRA federal and Quebec provincial tax brackets to estimate savings. It does not account for the 16.5% federal tax abatement Quebec residents receive, so federal tax estimates may be overstated. Results are estimates — consult Revenu Québec or a tax professional for personalized advice.
