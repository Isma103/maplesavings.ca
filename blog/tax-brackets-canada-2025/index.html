Canada's income tax system is progressive — you pay a higher rate only on the portion of income within each bracket, not on your total income. This guide covers all 2025 federal brackets and provincial top rates confirmed from CRA sources.

How Progressive Taxation Works

Many Canadians think their marginal rate applies to all their income — it doesn't. You pay the lowest rate on the first portion, progressively higher rates on higher portions.

Example: $100,000 federal taxable income (2025)
First $57,375 × 14.5%$8,319
Remaining $42,625 × 20.5%$8,738
Total federal tax$17,057

You do not pay 20.5% on all $100,000. The 20.5% rate only applies to income above $57,375. On top of this, the federal basic personal amount ($16,129) reduces your taxable income by that amount before tax is calculated.

2025 Federal Tax Brackets

Federal Basic Personal Amount (2025)

$16,129 — The first $16,129 of income is effectively tax-free federally. This is applied as a non-refundable tax credit at the lowest bracket rate.

Quebec Federal Abatement

Quebec residents receive a 16.5% federal tax abatement because Quebec administers its own provincial income tax and social programs separately. In practice, Quebec residents pay 83.5% of their calculated federal tax, then add Quebec's own provincial rates.

How RRSP Contributions Reduce Bracket Exposure

Every dollar you contribute to an RRSP reduces your taxable income by one dollar. This can:

  • Reduce the amount of income taxed at higher brackets
  • In some cases, move you into a lower bracket entirely
  • Save you taxes at your combined federal + provincial marginal rate
