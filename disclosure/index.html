Referral Disclosure

How our referral relationship with Wealthsimple works, and what conditions apply to any bonus offer.

Summary: Some links on MapleSavings.ca are referral links. If you open a Wealthsimple account through our link, both you and MapleSavings may receive a referral bonus — subject to Wealthsimple's current eligibility conditions. We are not affiliated with Wealthsimple or the Canada Revenue Agency.

Our Referral Relationship

MapleSavings uses a Wealthsimple referral link on this website. This means that if you click a link on MapleSavings.ca, open a Wealthsimple account, and meet Wealthsimple's current eligibility conditions, both you (the new client) and MapleSavings (the referrer) may receive a bonus from Wealthsimple.

This referral arrangement is how MapleSavings earns revenue. The calculator itself is free to use and there is no cost to you for visiting this site.

Wealthsimple Referral Offer — Conditions Apply

Wealthsimple's referral bonus is subject to their current program terms, which may change at any time. Before relying on any specific bonus amount or condition, please verify the current offer directly on Wealthsimple's website.

As of the time this page was last updated, the general structure of Wealthsimple's referral program includes conditions such as:

  • Both the new client and the referrer must have or open a Wealthsimple account
  • The new client must fund their account from an external source within a specified time period
  • A minimum deposit amount may be required to qualify for the bonus
  • Bonuses may be subject to a hold period — early withdrawal may result in clawback

For the most current and accurate offer terms, visit Wealthsimple directly: wealthsimple.com/invite/E7IMSP.

What We Recommend and Why

MapleSavings recommends Wealthsimple for RRSP, FHSA, and TFSA accounts because it is one of Canada's largest investing platforms, offers registered accounts with no account fees, is regulated as a securities dealer in Canada, and is a CIPF member (the Canadian Investor Protection Fund, which covers client assets in the event of a firm insolvency — it does not cover investment losses).

Our recommendation is based on these factors, not solely on the existence of a referral relationship. However, you should always compare platforms before opening an account and choose what is right for your personal financial situation.

Consult a qualified tax professional or financial advisor for advice tailored to your situation. MapleSavings is not affiliated with the Canada Revenue Agency or any government body.
