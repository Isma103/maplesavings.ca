Tax Refund Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

We total your income (T1 Line 15000), subtract your deductions to get Net Income (Line 23600) and Taxable Income (Line 26000), then calculate federal and provincial tax on that amount. Non-refundable credits reduce the tax owing. CPP and EI are added, and tax already withheld is subtracted — what's left is your estimated refund or balance owing.

Average refunds vary widely by income, province, and deductions claimed — there's no single official figure that applies to every filer. Your own refund depends on how much tax was withheld compared to your actual tax owing. Enter your numbers above for a personalized estimate. Once you know your refund, our investment growth calculator can show how it could grow if invested instead of spent.

The CRA typically issues refunds within about 2 weeks for electronically filed returns, and around 8 weeks for paper returns, assuming no review is required. Direct deposit is faster than a mailed cheque.

Common ways include contributing to your RRSP before the deadline, claiming all eligible deductions and credits (union dues, child care, tuition, medical expenses, donations), and making sure your employer isn't over-withholding. This calculator shows how an RRSP contribution could change your result — for the exact tax savings of a specific contribution amount, see our RRSP & FHSA Calculator.

A balance owing means less tax was withheld during the year than you actually owe. Contributing to your RRSP before the deadline reduces taxable income dollar-for-dollar at your marginal rate. If you owe more than $3,000, the CRA may also require quarterly instalments the following year.

Yes. CPP contributions and EI premiums are calculated automatically from your employment income using confirmed 2024 and 2025 CRA rates (2026 rates are indexed estimates), and both the amount payable and the corresponding tax credit are factored into your result.

This estimator does not model capital gains (the inclusion rate is currently uncertain), the OAS clawback, the Alternative Minimum Tax, the Ontario surtax, Quebec-specific QPIP credits, or refundable credits like the GST/HST credit. Dividends and self-employment CPP use simplified treatment.

This tool uses CRA-confirmed federal and provincial tax brackets, CPP and EI rates, and the most common non-refundable credits. It's directionally accurate for typical T4 employees and retirees, but your actual Notice of Assessment may differ. Always confirm your final result by filing your actual return.
