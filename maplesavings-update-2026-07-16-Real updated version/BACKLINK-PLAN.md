# BACKLINK-PLAN.md — Off-Page Authority Plan
*Produced: 2026-07-15 · Written because Ahrefs confirmed MapleSavings.ca has Domain Rating 0 — the site has zero backlink authority, which is the primary reason it ranks position 70-90 despite clean on-page SEO.*

---

## Why this matters more than any further on-page work

On-page fixes (titles, schema, internal links, new calculator pages) raise the *ceiling* a page can reach — they don't move a DR-0 site off page 7-9 of Google results by themselves. Backlinks from real, relevant sites are the actual lever. This plan is scoped to legitimate, sustainable tactics only — no link farms, PBNs, paid link schemes, or anything that risks a Google penalty. Every tactic below is something a small, honest site can realistically execute.

## Research notes (via web search, no Ahrefs access from this session)

The "best free Canadian tax calculator" space is genuinely crowded — a roundup search surfaced TaxTips.ca, BracketBuddy.ca (our named competitor), TurboTax, WOWA.ca, TaxesCalculator.ca, MyTaxCalculator.ca, CloudTax, and Loonies & Sense all already ranking for that exact query. Getting added to an *existing* roundup post is realistic; ranking a *new* roundup post above these established players is not, in the near term. The pitch angle that differentiates MapleSavings — dedicated pages for all 13 provinces/territories, an FHSA calculator (several competitors lack one), and 2024/2025/2026 brackets confirmed against CRA — should be the hook in every outreach message, not "we're a tax calculator too."

## Tier 2 — Organic community participation (slow, compounding, zero cost)

| Target | Approach |
|---|---|
| r/PersonalFinanceCanada, r/cantax, r/CanadaFinance | **Do not self-promote directly.** Answer real questions genuinely (tax bracket questions, RRSP vs FHSA questions come up constantly); link to a specific relevant calculator page only when it's a natural, helpful answer — not every reply. Most subreddits cap self-promotion at a low ratio (commonly cited as roughly 1-in-10 posts); violating this gets a domain banned outright, which would be worse than not trying. |
| Canadian personal finance Discord/forum communities | Same rule — participate first, link rarely and only when relevant. |

## Tier 3 — Journalist / media source pitching

Canadian tax season (Feb-April) generates heavy demand from journalists for sources and illustrative numbers. Named columnists who regularly cover this beat:

- **Jamie Golombek** — Financial Post
- **Erica Alini** — Maclean's
- **Clare O'Hara** — The Globe and Mail (wealth management)
- **Rob Carrick** — The Globe and Mail (personal finance)

Platform: [Qwoted](https://www.qwoted.com/) connects sources to journalists directly — worth creating a free source profile. [ResponseSource](https://www.responsesource.com/) runs a similar journalist-enquiry service.

**Pitch angle:** not "please link to us" — offer to be a data/illustration source for a tax-season story (e.g., "here's what a $75k Ontario salary saves with an FHSA vs RRSP contribution, happy to run other scenarios"). A citation with a link is the natural byproduct of being a useful source, not the ask itself.

## What this plan deliberately excludes

- Paid link placements or link-buying services (Google penalty risk, and dishonest)
- Automated directory-submission tools (mostly low-quality/spam directories that provide no real value and can look manipulative)
- Any subreddit/forum posting that isn't a genuine, on-topic, rare mention
- Fabricated testimonials, fake "featured on" badges, or claiming media coverage that hasn't happened

## Suggested next step

Start with Tier 1 (lowest effort, existing infrastructure) and Tier 3 (highest leverage if it lands — a single Globe and Mail or Financial Post citation is worth more than dozens of directory links). Tier 2 is a background, ongoing habit rather than a one-time task. Re-check Ahrefs DR monthly to see if anything is moving the needle.
