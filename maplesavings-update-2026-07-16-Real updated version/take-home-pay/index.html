Take-Home Pay Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

Take-home pay is your gross salary minus federal income tax, provincial or territorial income tax, CPP or QPP contributions (including the CPP2/QPP2 second contribution), EI premiums, and in Quebec, QPIP premiums. This calculator applies 2025 CRA tax brackets with the basic personal amount credit, then subtracts 2025 payroll deductions to estimate your net annual, monthly and bi-weekly pay.

CPP2 is the second additional CPP contribution introduced in 2024. For 2025, employees contribute 4% on earnings between $71,300 (the first earnings ceiling) and $81,200 (the second earnings ceiling), up to a maximum of $396.00. It applies on top of the base CPP contribution of 5.95% up to $71,300.

Quebec employees pay into QPP instead of CPP (6.4% vs 5.95% in 2025), pay a lower EI rate (1.31% vs 1.64%) because Quebec runs its own parental insurance plan, and additionally pay QPIP premiums (0.494% up to $98,000 of earnings). Quebec residents also receive a 16.5% federal tax abatement, which reduces federal tax, while Quebec provincial tax rates are among the highest in Canada.

Your marginal tax rate is the combined federal + provincial rate applied to your next dollar of income. Your average tax rate is your total income tax divided by your gross salary — always lower than your marginal rate because lower brackets and the basic personal amount shelter part of your income.

RRSP contributions are deducted from your taxable income for both federal and provincial tax, so every dollar contributed saves tax at roughly your marginal rate. They do not reduce CPP/QPP or EI premiums, which are based on gross employment earnings. Enter an RRSP amount in the calculator to see your estimated annual tax savings.

For 2025, the maximum employee CPP contribution is $4,034.10 (5.95% on pensionable earnings between $3,500 and $71,300), plus a maximum CPP2 contribution of $396.00 (4% between $71,300 and $81,200). The maximum EI premium is $1,077.48 outside Quebec (1.64% up to $65,700 of insurable earnings) and $860.67 in Quebec (1.31%). Quebec's QPP maximum is $4,339.20 plus $396.00 QPP2, and the QPIP maximum is $484.12.
